Why I built it

Last summer my neighbor messaged me at 10:30 PM asking where he could leave bags near the main train station for four hours — his bus to Vienna was at 6 AM the next day. I opened Google, the ZSSK website, Bounce, Stasher and Radical Storage. Each one only showed their own listings. No comparison. No comprehensive view.

So I made a map with my own notes. I shared it with friends from high school, and suddenly people I didn't know were using it. That's when I made it public.

How it works

LockAndGo is not a storage company. I'm a discovery layer — I aggregate 32 spots across Bratislava (Bounce partners, Radical Storage, LuggageHero, ZSSK manual storage and 24/7 lockers, self-service boxes at OC Nivy, cloakrooms at Aupark and Vivo!, hotel storage rooms). Click a spot, see details (price, hours, location photo), then book directly with the operator.

The price you pay is the same as if you booked directly. I don't add a markup. Bounce, Radical Storage, LuggageHero and Stasher pay me a commission for bookings routed through my site. The other spots (ZSSK, Nivy boxes, Aupark cloakroom, hotel storage, walk-in shops) are listed for free — no commission, but user trust matters more to me than a few extra euros. If the cheapest option isn't an affiliate, I'll still tell you about it.

Transparently: Out of 32 spots, I earn affiliate commission on 12 (Bounce, Radical Storage, LuggageHero — global networks). The other 20 spots pay me nothing — they're listed because they make sense for travelers. Commission rates run 5–15% of booking value depending on the partner. That's my entire revenue from the site.

Who it helps

  • Tourists arriving from Vienna (BTS airport, Main Station, Mlynské Nivy bus station) — no desire to drag suitcases through Old Town.
  • Day-trippers from Vienna or Budapest — drop bags in the morning, pick up before evening departure.
  • Transit travelers Vienna ↔ Budapest/Prague stopping in Bratislava for 2–4 hours.
  • Slovaks before flights — BTS airport has no proper storage; you store in town first.
  • Locals moving apartments, waiting for housing, or that neighbor at 10:30 PM.
